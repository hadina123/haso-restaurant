  1. For the rice:

  2. 3 cups basmati rice (rinsed and soaked for at least 30 mins)

  3. 1 tbsp salt

  4. Water for boiling

  1. For the meat:

  2. 1.5 to 2 lbs (700–900g) lamb or beef (bone-in preferred)

  3. medium onions (sliced)

  4. 4–5 garlic cloves smashed

  8. 1/2 tsp ground cardamom

  9. 1/2 tsp turmeric

  10. Salt to taste

  11. 3 tbsp oil

  12. 3 cups water (or enough to cook the meat)

  1. For the topping:

  2. 2 medium carrots cut into matchsticks

  3. 1/2 cup raisins

  4. 1/2 tsp sugar (optional)

  5. 2 tbsp oil or butter

    6. :
  6. 1/4 cup slivered almonds or pistachios (optional)

---
    Instructions:
  • Cook the meat: In a large pot, heat oil and sauté onions until golden. Add meat and sear until browned on all sides. Add garlic and spices; stir for a minute. Pour water, bring to a boil, then simmer (covered) for ~1.5 hours or until tender. Once cooked, remove the meat and save the broth.

  • Prepare the rice: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add soaked rice and boil until it’s just shy of fully cooked (about 6–8 mins). Drain and set aside.

  • Make the topping: In a pan, heat oil/butter and sauté carrots until soft. Add raisins and sugar (if using), cook until they plump. Optionally add nuts at the end for crunch.

  • Layer and steam: In a large pot, layer some rice, then the meat, then more rice on top. Sprinkle a bit of the meat broth between layers. Make 3–4 holes with the handle of a spoon for steam to circulate. Cover the pot lid with a clean towel and seal tightly. Steam on low heat for 30–45 minutes.

  • Assemble and serve: Gently mix rice before serving, plate with meat and toppings spread over the top. Serve with salad, chutney, or yogurt on the side.
